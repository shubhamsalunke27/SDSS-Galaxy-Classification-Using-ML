About This Project

The SDSS Galaxy Classification System is an advanced machine learning application designed to classify galaxies from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) into two distinct categories: STARFORMING and STARBURST galaxies.

Project Overview

This project leverages astronomical data from one of the most comprehensive sky surveys ever conducted. The SDSS DR18 dataset contains measurements of over 100,000 galaxies, including their magnitudes, fluxes, physical sizes, and redshift values. Our machine learning model has been trained to identify patterns that distinguish actively star-forming galaxies from those experiencing intense starburst events.

The Science Behind It

STARFORMING galaxies have a normal, sustained rate of star formation. They continuously produce new stars at a relatively constant pace over billions of years.

STARBURST galaxies are undergoing rapid, intense periods of star formation, often triggered by galactic collisions or interactions. These events create massive numbers of new stars in a relatively short time span.

Model Architecture

Our classification system uses a Random Forest Classifier with 100 decision trees. This ensemble method combines multiple decision trees to make robust predictions with high accuracy. The model was trained on a balanced dataset using cross-validation techniques to ensure generalization.

Input Features

The classifier uses 10 astronomical parameters derived from SDSS measurements:

Magnitudes (u, g, r, i, z)

Brightness measurements in different wavelength bands representing the galaxy's spectral energy distribution

Model Fluxes

Light intensity measurements in specific bands (i-band and z-band) used for stellar mass estimation

Petrosian Radii

Physical size measurements of galaxies in multiple bands, important for morphological classification

Redshift (z)

Measure of how far the galaxy is from Earth, related to its age and distance
